# Case Study on Phishing

**Phishing** is one of the most common types of cybercrime in which attackers deceive people into sharing confidential information such as usernames, passwords, bank account details, debit/credit card numbers, or OTPs. The attacker usually pretends to be a trusted person or organization, such as a bank, online shopping site, payment app, or government service.

A realistic example is of a college student named Rahul, who received an email that appeared to be from his bank. The email warned him that his account would be suspended unless he verified his details immediately. Since the message looked professional and included the bank’s logo, Rahul believed it was genuine.

The phishing attack happened step-by-step in the following way. First, the cybercriminal created a fake email that closely resembled an official bank email. Second, the message was sent to Rahul with urgent language to create panic. Third, the email included a link that redirected him to a fake banking website designed to look exactly like the original one. Fourth, Rahul entered his login ID, password, and OTP on the fake page. Finally, the attacker used those details to access his bank account and transfer money without his permission.

Phishing usually targets students, online shoppers, bank customers, elderly people, and employees who may not carefully verify online messages. The consequences can include financial loss, stolen personal data, unauthorized transactions, and identity theft.

This case highlights that phishing succeeds mainly because of trust, urgency, and lack of awareness. It can be prevented by checking the sender carefully, avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing passwords or OTPs online.
